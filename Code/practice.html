Approach to Practice

The practices on this page are meant to be simple starting points rather than advanced techniques. Each one focuses on combining posture, breathing, and attention so that training feels steady instead of rushed.

These examples are inspired by experiences in Shitō-ryū training and adapted into a format that anyone can read and reflect on, regardless of rank or previous experience.

Breathing Drill: Four-Count Calm

This breathing drill uses a simple four-count rhythm: inhale for four counts, hold for four, exhale for four, and rest for four. Practicing this while standing in a relaxed stance can help quiet the nervous system before more intense training.

Stance Focus: Shiko-Dachi

Shiko-dachi is a wide, grounded stance that builds leg strength and stability. Holding the stance with an upright spine and steady breathing can be a way to practice staying calm under physical effort, rather than tensing up or rushing.

Standing Meditation

A brief standing meditation before or after training involves placing the feet shoulder-width apart, softening the knees, and resting the hands gently in front of the body. The goal is to notice the breath and posture without forcing anything to change.
